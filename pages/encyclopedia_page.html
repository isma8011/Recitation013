Interpreter of Maladies by Jhumpa Lahiri

A collection of stories about relationships, community, and culture.

Interpreter of Maladies is a book written by Jhumpa Lahiri that was published in 1999. It is a fictional series of short stories that share a common theme centered around Lahiri's Indian heritage. Though each story focuses on a different set of characters, they are tied together by the overarching emotional connections the author highlights through beautifully written prose. It won the Pulitizer Prize for Fiction after being published, a testament to the moving work of art Lahiri produced.

About the Author


By librairie mollat, CC BY 3.0, Link

Jhumpa Lahiri was born in London, but raised in Rhode Island, the daughter of Bengali immigrants. The experiences of the characters in Interpreter of Maladies draw much inspiration from her own childhood and upbringing, where she often felt torn between her heritage and the country she was growing up in. Despite this, she pursued multiple degrees in English and literature, eventually becoming a professor at Princeton University.
